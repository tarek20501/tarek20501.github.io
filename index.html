About Me

I was born and raised in Syria, specifically in the city of Homs. Ever since I was a child, I have enjoyed problem solving. For instance, whenever I experience a problem in my computer's or smartphone's software, I systematically investigate it and try to come up with a solution.

Given my interest in problem solving, I knew that I wanted to study engineering when I was in high school. I finished high school in Jordan in 2014 because my family had to relocate in 2013 due to the Syrian Civil War. When I graduated from high school, I chose Biomedical Engineering

In 2014, I enrolled in Jordan University of Science and Technology in the Biomedical Engineering Program. I excelled in my first year, and at the end of term 1 of my second year (late 2015), my family received an offer to immigrate to Canada, which they accepted.

I arrived in Vancouver, Canada in January 2016, and I had to adapt quickly to this new world to continue pursuing my education. By June 2016 I acquired my IELTS certificate and met the English requirement at the University of British Columbia. In September 2016, I started my first year of engineering at UBC. Then, In September 2017, I started studying in the Electrical Engineering program with the Biomedical option. Lastly, I am expected to finish this program in May 2020.
